ABOUT


GOODS AND SERVICES TAX is an indirect tax which was introduced in India on 1 July 2017
by the President of India, Pranab Mukherjee, and Prime Minister of India, Narendra Modi and
was applicable throughout India which replaced multiple cascading taxes levied by the central
and state governments. GST is levied on all transactions such as sale,transfer,purchase,barter,
lease etc. There are three applicable taxes uner GST: CGST, SGST and IGST. GST is administered
by both the Union and State Governments. The Goods and Services Tax (GST) will be levied at
multiple rates ranging from 0 per cent to 28 per cent. GST Council finalised a four-tier GST tax
structure of 5%, 12%, 18% and 28%, with lower rates for essential items and the highest for
luxury and de-merits goods that would also attract an additional cess

Introduction of GST would be a very significant step in the field of indirect tax reforms in India.
By amalgamating a large number of Central and State taxes into a single tax and allowing
set-off of prior-stage taxes, it would mitigate the ill effects of cascading and pave the way for a
common national market. GST will improve the collection of taxes as well as boost
the development of Indian economy by removing the indirect tax barriers between states and
integrating the country thorugh a uniform tax rate.
